I received my B.Eng. and M.Sc. degrees from Shenzhen University, Shenzhen, China, in 2015 and 2018, respectively, both in Electrical and Electronic Engineering. From July 2018 to November 2018, I worked as a research assistant at Southern University of Science and Technology, Shenzhen, China. In December 2018, I joined the Signal Processing Group at Darmstadt University of Technology, Darmstadt, Germany, whose official name is Technische Universität Darmstadt (TU Darmstadt), and commenced working on my doctoral degree.

My research interests lie in statistical and array signal processing, including direction-of-arrival (DOA) estimation, source localization, robust adaptive beamforming, sparse array design, compressed sensing, and optimization theory, with applications to radar, sonar, navigation, wireless communications, microphone array processing and so on.

I serve as a technical reviewer for several peer-reviewed journals and conferences, including but not limited to IEEE Transactions on Aerospace and Electronic Systems, IEEE Signal Processing Letters, IEEE Wireless Communications Letters, Signal Processing (Elsevier), Digital Signal Processing (Elsevier), IET Signal Processing, IET Communications, Multidimensional Systems and Signal Processing (Springer), and IEEE Sensor Array and Multichannel Signal Processing Workshop.
